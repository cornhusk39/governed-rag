PART I

Item 1. Business

Northwind Trading Co. is a diversified distributor of specialty food and beverage products, serving grocery, food-service, and e-commerce channels across North America and Europe. The Company sources from a broad base of producers and operates a network of regional distribution centers.

The Company's reportable segments are Grocery Distribution, Food Service, and Direct-to-Consumer. Competition is based on product selection, price, reliability of fulfillment, and the strength of supplier relationships. The Company believes its scale and logistics capabilities provide durable advantages.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

An investment in the Company's securities involves risk. Perishable inventory exposes the Company to spoilage and write-downs if demand forecasts prove inaccurate. Concentration among a small number of large customers means the loss of any one could materially reduce revenue.

The Company depends on third-party carriers, and disruptions in transportation networks could increase costs or delay deliveries. Currency fluctuations affect the reported results of the Company's European operations.

Item 2. Properties

The Company leases its corporate headquarters and owns or leases regional distribution centers totaling approximately four million square feet. Management believes the Company's facilities are adequate for current needs and suitable for expansion.

Item 3. Legal Proceedings

From time to time the Company is party to litigation arising in the ordinary course of business. The Company does not believe that the outcome of any currently pending matter will be material to its financial position.

Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

The Company is exposed to interest-rate risk on its variable-rate borrowings and to foreign-currency risk on its European operations. The Company uses forward contracts from time to time to manage a portion of its currency exposure.
